Accomodation

Here's a broad description of Indy's areas:


Downtown Indy is where the city center is and also where we live. The most happening part of downtown is Mass Ave where you can find several restaurants and craft cocktail bars as well as a lot of hotels and airbnbs. We have a room block at Bottleworks Hotel for June 23rd under the name "Acharya-Chanana".


Broad Ripple has a free-spirited vibe, with laid-back cafes, global eateries, lively brewpubs and happening clubs. The popular Monon Trail runs through this area.

Fountain Square is a quirky neighborhood with several galleries, a vintage duckpin bowling alley, laid-back eateries, offbeat nightlife including music cafes and burlesque clubs. This is also near where a lot of HGTV shows are filmed.

Brownsburg, Whitestown, and Avon are the suburbs of Indianapolis close to where the reception dinner is. If you're over 40, we recommend staying here.

Carmel and Zionsville are rich suburbs of Indy that are far from the venue. They have some cute stuff.

Please text us if you have any specific questions about where to stay.

Transportation

We like to say everything in and around Indianapolis is ~20 mins away from each other so please rent a car.

Food and Drinks

Here are some of our favorites:

Bodhi - Thai restaurant on Mass Ave. In-person waitlist only but, the food is authentic and the vibe is instagram-worthy.
Diavola - Neapolitan style pizza place. Deepa's favorite is the Mais Burrata while Shaurya's is the Truffle Funghi.
Bosphorus - Turkish restaurant near Fountain Square. We recommend getting The Bosphorus Special.
Amberson Coffee & Grocer - Best coffee in Indianapolis; think Portland hipster vibes. We highly recommend their Eggy sandwich.
Leviathan Bakehouse - Coffee shop and bakery with good looking (and tasting) viennoiserie. They also have good sandwiches.
Ball & Biscuit - Craft cocktail bar on Mass Ave. Deepa spent many of her days here before (old man) Shaurya moved to Indy and then we stopped going there because it's loud.

Things to do in Indy

Indianapolis Museum of Art - The Indianapolis Museum of Art is an encyclopedic art museum located at Newfields, a 152-acre campus that includes art galleries, lush gardens, performance spaces, a nature preserve, and sculpture park. (We really enjoyed this place)

Indianapolis Zoo - The Indianapolis Zoo is a 64-acre zoo located in White River State Park, in Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. The Indianapolis Zoo is home to more than 1,200 animals of approximately 230 species and subspecies, including 16 endangered species, and three species that are extinct in the wild. (We've never been so don't quote us on this)

Indianapolis Motor Speedway - The Indianapolis Motor Speedway is an automobile racing circuit located in Speedway, Indiana, in the United States. It is the home of the Indianapolis 500 and the Brickyard 400, and formerly the home of the United States Grand Prix. It is located on the corner of 16th Street and Georgetown Road, approximately six miles west of Downtown Indianapolis. (same disclaimer as above)

Indianapolis Cultural Trail - The Indianapolis Cultural Trail is an 8-mile urban pedestrian and bike path in downtown Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. The trail connects neighborhoods, Cultural Districts and entertainment amenities, and serves as the downtown hub for central Indiana's vast greenway system.

Monon Trail - The Monon Trail is a rail trail located entirely within the state of Indiana. The Monon Railroad was a popular railroad line connecting the cities of Chicago and Indianapolis, with stops at major settlements along its route. After the decline of railroad travel and the sale of the company, the section of the former railroad between Indianapolis and Delphi, Indiana, was donated to the state of Indiana for use as a trail. The trail is named for the Monon Railroad, which previously owned the corridor.
